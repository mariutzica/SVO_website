theophylline

matter

Definition: Theophylline, also known as 1,3-dimethylxanthine, is a phosphodiesterase inhibiting drug used in therapy for respiratory diseases such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and asthma under a variety of brand names. As a member of the xanthine family, it bears structural and pharmacological similarity to theobromine and caffeine, and is present intea (Camellia sinensis). A small amount of theophylline is one of the products of caffeine metabolic processing in the liver.
